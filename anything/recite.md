## Some conclusion
$when\; n \to +\infty:$
$$
    if~ a = -1,~ then~ 1 + \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} + ... + \frac{1}{n}~ \sim~ \ln{n}\\
    if~ a > -1,~ then~ 1^a + 2^a +3^a + ... + n^a~ \sim~ \frac{1}{a + 1}n^{a+1}\\
    if~ a > -1,~ then~ 1^a + 2^a +3^a + ... + n^a~ converge
$$
## Trigonometric identities
$$
    \sin^3\theta = \frac{3\sin{\theta} - \sin{3\theta}}{4}\\
    \cos^3\theta = \frac{3\cos{\theta} + \cos{3\theta}}{4}\\
    \sin{\alpha}\cos{\beta} = \frac{\sin{(\alpha + \beta)} + \sin{(\alpha - \beta)}}{2}\\
    \cos{\alpha}\cos{\beta} = \frac{\cos{(\alpha + \beta)} + \cos{(\alpha - \beta)}}{2}\\
    \sin{\alpha}\sin{\beta} = -\frac{\cos{(\alpha + \beta)} - \cos{(\alpha - \beta)}}{2}\\
$$
